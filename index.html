A clear objective

The goal of the visualisation is to help decision-makers take the best course of action.

It is critical to understand the actions available, the question asked and the limitations of the data. Only with that information in mind can we then choose the proper chart type, the overall dashboard design, interactivity, alerts, schedules and all the UX/UI. These three pillars will get us half the way:
- Communicate often. Understand the business context.
- Fail fast. Sketch and prototype. Obtain a minimum viable product.
- Iterate. Incorporate feedback and plan the new releases.

View Works

Legacy systems, Disjoint processes

I'm yet to find a project where "data is ready". More often than not, data will need to be extracted, transformed and loaded before it can be used to make the critical decisions that need to be made.

Databases provide a centralized, curated datasource, whereas flat files provide with flexibility to regular business users. Cloud solutions abound, and API are just one click —or a few lines of code— away.

Crucially, automation of repeated processes decrease human error while freeing up business resources.

We specialize in handling all of of the above while maintaining handover to business user painless.
